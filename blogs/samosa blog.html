Indian samosa is a dish as diverse as India itself.


The shape and the pastry are among the only constants, to be fair though, even the pastry changes a little bit depending on the area and region.


Which is why, in the modern day, the word samosa refers more to an entire family of pastries rather than one singular food item


In Central Asia, for instance, where they are still called samsa, crusts are thicker and crumblier, and fillings are traditionally meatier, with mince and onion, as in the early days.


Samosa can be eaten during any time of the day like breakfast, lunch, or in the evening .
